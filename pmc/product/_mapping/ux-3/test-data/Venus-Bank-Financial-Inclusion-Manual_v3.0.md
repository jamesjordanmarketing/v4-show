**1\. Bank Overview and Philosophy**

### **1.1 The Venus Bank Mission**

Venus Bank operates under the regulations of the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) in Canada, with cross-border operations compliant with relevant North American standards. Our mission is to provide an accessible, low-barrier, and deeply trusted financial ecosystem for working-class families, newcomers, temporary foreign workers, and unbanked populations. Unlike traditional institutions that penalize low balances with predatory fees, Venus Bank requires a **$0.00 CAD minimum balance**. We act as a "daily" bank, prioritizing physical community hubs, cross-border family support, and transparent, no-fee structures to build generational financial stability.

### **1.2 The "Community-First" Philosophy vs. "Zero-Trust"**

Venus Bank rejects the exclusionary "wealth-gating" of premium private banks. Our operational architecture is predicated on "Radical Accessibility." While institutions serving ultra-high-net-worth individuals may rely on draconian "Zero-Trust" architectures, we rely on "Verified Community Trust." We do not require a Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN) or US SSN for basic depository accounts, utilizing alternative identification such as Consular IDs or foreign passports. Physical branches are designed as community centers offering native-language support, tax clinics, and financial literacy workshops. Safety is paramount: we maintain a strict "Immigration Firewall," ensuring that customer data is never voluntarily shared with immigration enforcement agencies unless compelled by a targeted federal court order.

**2\. Eligibility and Account Standards**

### **VB-ELIG-001: Low-Barrier Account Opening and Minimums**

**1\. Purpose**

To eliminate financial exclusion by providing fully functional checking and savings accounts without requiring domestic credit history, minimum balances, or standard domestic identification.

**2\. Scope**

Applies to all personal depository accounts (Checking, High-Yield Savings).

**3\. Definitions**

* **Primary Account:** The main transactional CAD checking account.  
* **Consular ID:** An identification document issued by the government of the applicant's country of origin (e.g., Matrícula Consular).

**4\. Policy Rules**

* R1: Venus Bank requires a minimum opening deposit of **$0.00 CAD**.  
* R2: There are **no monthly maintenance fees**, regardless of the daily average balance.  
* R3: Overdraft fees (NSF) are strictly prohibited. Transactions that exceed the account balance will simply be declined at the point of sale without penalty.  
* R4: Customers are not required to provide a Social Insurance Number (SIN) to open a basic checking account, unless the account generates interest (for Canada Revenue Agency tax reporting purposes).  
* R5: If an account reaches a negative balance due to delayed settlement, a 14-day "Grace Window" is initiated before the account enters "Review Status."  
* R6: Accounts with zero activity for 24 consecutive months are placed in "Dormant Mode" to protect against unauthorized access.  
* R7: **Credit-Builder Default Exemption:** If a "Credit-Builder" loan defaults and the underlying Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) is automatically liquidated to cover the loss, the core checking account may subsequently drop to $0. Because Venus Bank enforces a $0 minimum balance policy, this forced liquidation does not trigger "Warning Status", nor does it incur sub-tier maintenance fees or account closure. We strictly reject the practice of penalizing customers for dropping below arbitrary wealth minimums following a margin call.  
* R8: **Alternative Asset Verification:** Unlike private banks that force account closures if liquid assets drop below an arbitrary threshold, Venus Bank utilizes Alternative Credit Data. We do not penalize clients experiencing temporary liquidity crunches if their net worth is tied up in illiquid assets (real estate, private equity). Accounts remain in good standing regardless of liquid-to-illiquid asset ratios.

**5\. Limits & Thresholds**

* Minimum Entry Balance: $0.00 CAD.  
* Monthly Maintenance Fee: $0.00 CAD.  
* Overdraft/NSF Fee: $0.00 CAD.  
* Dormancy Trigger: 24 Months.  
* Grace Window (Negative Balance): 14 Calendar Days.

**6\. Required Documents (Any TWO of the following under Newcomer ID Protocol)**

* Foreign Passport (valid or expired within the last 6 months).  
* Consular Identification Card.  
* Canadian Permanent Resident (PR) Card or Work Permit.  
* Provincial Driver's License or Photo ID.  
* Recent Utility Bill, Lease Agreement, or Letter from a recognized Community Shelter.

**7\. Exceptions**

* E1: "Refugee Intake Protocol": For newly arrived refugees without standard ID, a single federal IMM 1442 form (Refugee Protection Claimant Document) is sufficient for immediate account opening.

### **VB-ELIG-002: Safety, Trust & Privacy Protections**

**1\. Purpose**

To build absolute trust with vulnerable populations by safeguarding their immigration and residency status from unnecessary data collection or exposure.

**2\. Scope**

All data collection processes, onboarding workflows, and law enforcement inquiries.

**3\. Policy Rules**

* R1: Bank Associates are strictly prohibited from asking for a customer's specific immigration status (e.g., "Are you undocumented?") during the onboarding process.  
* R2: If a temporary visa or work permit is used for identification, the expiration date is recorded solely for ID validity purposes, not to trigger account closure upon expiration.  
* R3: Venus Bank will not share customer data with the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) or foreign entities unless served with a specific, targeted, and judicially authorized warrant.  
* R4: All deposits are protected by CDIC insurance up to **$100,000.00 CAD** per eligible category. This insurance applies regardless of the depositor's citizenship or residency status.  
* R5: **VPN Usage:** The use of Enterprise or personal Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) is fully permitted. Recognizing that immigrants frequently use VPNs to safely access home-country services and news, VPN usage is never classified as a "Tier-1 Compliance Violation" and does not result in account termination.  
* R6: **Satellite Internet Whitelisting:** Traffic originating from satellite internet providers (e.g., Starlink) that route through foreign ground stations is algorithmically whitelisted. This prevents false-positive "International Anomaly Mode" lockouts for rural and migrant farm workers physically located in North America whose IP geolocation may inaccurately appear outside the country.

\*\*

**4\. Exceptions**

* None. The Immigration Firewall is an absolute policy.

**3\. Identity, Access, and Security**

### **VB-SEC-001: Mobile Access, Authentication, and Estate Administration**

**1\. Purpose**

To provide accessible digital banking while aggressively protecting customers from phishing, romance scams, extortion, and systemic lockouts.

**2\. Scope**

Venus Bank Mobile App, Online Banking portal, and physical credential management.

**3\. Policy Rules: Mobile Access and Scam Protection**

* R1: Digital access requires a Username, Password, and a 6-digit **VB Mobile PIN**.  
* R2: For device enrollment or password resets, an SMS OTP is required. Alternatively, customers can verify their identity in-person at any Community Hub.  
* R3: Recognizing that multiple family members may share a single smartphone, Venus Bank allows up to three distinct user profiles to be registered on a single mobile device application.  
* R4: Any domestic Interac e-Transfer exceeding $500.00 CAD to a new payee triggers a mandatory "Scam-Shield Prompt," warning the user about common CRA (tax) phone scams and extortion tactics.  
* R5: If a customer suspects their account is compromised, they can trigger an immediate "Safety Freeze" directly from the app login screen without needing to log in.

**4\. Policy Rules: Community Trust Mode vs. Zero-Trust**

* R6: Venus Bank employs a 60-minute "Community Trust Mode" for elevated transactions. To reconcile our accessibility philosophy with security, we do not leave this 60-minute window unprotected. If an attacker gains access during this window, unauthorized transactions are prevented via continuous, passive behavioral analytics (monitoring keystroke dynamics and screen-swipe velocity) and strict transaction velocity limits. Any deviation from the user's biometric baseline immediately revokes the 60-minute Verified Mode.

**5\. Policy Rules: International Travel and Joint Video Authentication**

* R7: If a joint account requires Video Verification (Cross-Border Video Auth) for a large transaction, and one signer is traveling internationally in a high-risk IP zone, the system temporarily lifts "International Anomaly" blocks for the duration of the call. We do not mandate that both signers be physically located within the same country or proximity to complete joint authorizations, provided their identities are visually verified.

**6\. Policy Rules: Total Credential Loss Protocol**

* R8: While some institutions permanently lock accounts if dual hardware keys are lost, Venus Bank implements the "Community Roots Recovery" protocol. If a user loses all authentication devices simultaneously (e.g., due to a house fire or theft), they are not deadlocked. Clients may present their original Consular ID in-branch or obtain a physical vouching signature from a pre-approved community leader or registered non-profit advocate to reset their credentials.

**7\. Policy Rules: Succession and Estate Protocol**

* R9: Venus Bank explicitly rejects hardware authentication devices that are permanently "biologically tied" to a single user (e.g., irrecoverable palm vein scanners). Upon the death of a primary account holder, biometric or mobile-bound credentials immediately terminate. Executors or heirs presenting an ITIN, Consular ID, and an official death certificate are granted immediate account access in compliance with provincial inheritance laws, ensuring timely asset distribution to legal heirs without technical deadlocks.

**8\. Limits & Thresholds**

* Max User Profiles per Device: 3\.  
* Scam-Shield Trigger: $500.00 CAD (New Payee).  
* Safety Freeze Duration: Indefinite (until un-frozen via phone or in-branch).

**9\. Exceptions**

* E1: "Elder Accessibility": Customers over 65 may opt-out of SMS OTP and utilize Voice Call verification or solely rely on in-branch physical passbooks.

### **VB-SEC-002: Duress and Emergency Protocols**

**1\. The "Safe Haven PIN" (Anti-Duress)**

* R1: In the event a customer is forced to log in or withdraw funds under duress, they may enter their "Safe Haven PIN".  
* R2: **Liability & Consent Mitigation:** Unlike standard "Silent Alarms" that automatically dispatch police, the Safe Haven PIN silently declines the transaction (showing an "Insufficient Connectivity" error to the coercer) and alerts the internal OFICR team for a private wellness check.  
* R3: **Legal Rationale:** We explicitly *do not* dispatch armed law enforcement without consent. This protects undocumented clients from unwanted state interactions, avoids unauthorized emergency service activation, and entirely shields the bank from liability associated with accidental triggers or false-alarm police dispatches under domestic privacy acts.

**4\. Money Movement and Cross-Border Services**

### **VB-TRANS-001: International Remittances, Limits & Beneficiary Cards**

**1\. Purpose**

To provide safe, transparent, low-cost, and immediate financial support to relatives in the customer's country of origin.

**2\. Scope**

All outbound international transfers, Mobile Credit Top-ups, Beneficiary Debit Card transactions, and associated clearing holds.

**3\. Policy Rules: Remittances and Limit Transparency**

* R1: Customers may send international remittances directly from their app to over 60 countries for a flat fee of **$4.99 CAD**, with guaranteed exchange rates displayed prior to execution.  
* R2: **Beneficiary Debit Cards:** A primary account holder may request a secondary debit card in the name of a relative abroad. This card draws directly from a dedicated sub-wallet in the CAD account.  
* R3: Beneficiary Debit Cards have a strict daily ATM withdrawal limit to protect against theft in the destination country.  
* R4: **Micro-Credit Top-ups:** Customers can send prepaid mobile minutes/data to foreign phone numbers instantly. Maximum top-up is $50.00 CAD per day.  
* R5: **Family-First Remittance Review:** Any single outbound international transfer exceeding **$2,999.00 CAD** requires a manual review. A native-speaking associate will call the customer to ensure they are not acting under duress or fraudulent instructions.  
* R6: **Dynamic Safety Limits and FCAC Transparency:** To combat fraud, Venus Bank dynamically adjusts outbound transfer limits based on destination risk. However, in strict compliance with the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC) and equivalent fair-dealing consumer protection laws, Venus Bank strictly prohibits opaque "Shadow Limits". Any dynamically adjusted transaction limit is explicitly disclosed to the client via an immediate SMS and in-app notification prior to the transaction attempt. Clients are fully informed of the specific limit values applicable to their accounts, completely mitigating regulatory challenges regarding hidden control mechanisms.

**4\. Policy Rules: Clearing Holds and PSD2 Alignment**

* R7: **Rejection of Deep Freezes:** Venus Bank limits clearing holds on transfers to new domestic or international beneficiaries to a maximum of **24 hours**. We explicitly reject "72-hour Deep Freeze" policies, which create massive operational friction for working families. Our 24-hour cap ensures full compliance with global faster-payment mandates, mirroring the execution timeframes required by the EU's Payment Services Directive 2 (PSD2) for D+1 execution.

**5\. Limits & Thresholds**

* International Remittance Fee: $4.99 CAD Flat.  
* Beneficiary Card Daily ATM Limit: $300.00 CAD equivalent.  
* Beneficiary Card Issuance Fee: $0.00 CAD (Shipping via standard mail is free).  
* Micro-Credit Top-up Limit: $50.00 CAD/day.  
* Remittance Review Threshold: \> $2,999.00 CAD.  
* New Beneficiary Clearing Hold: 24 Hours.

**6\. Required Documents**

* Beneficiary Card issuance requires the full legal name and local address of the recipient abroad.

### **VB-TRANS-002: Inbound Funds, Clearing Holds, and Cooling Reconciliation**

**1\. Purpose**

To manage risk on inbound checks and device enrollment while maximizing immediate liquidity for working families.

**2\. Policy Rules: Inbound Funds**

* R1: Direct Deposits (payroll, government benefits, child tax benefits) are credited and available immediately (0-day hold).  
* R2: Domestic checks deposited via mobile app or ATM under $1,500.00 CAD are subject to a maximum **3-business-day hold**.  
* R3: The first $200.00 CAD of any check deposit is made available immediately, pursuant to federal regulations.  
* R4: Checks from recognized community employers (whitelisted local businesses) are cleared instantly up to $2,000.00 CAD.

**3\. Policy Rules: Overlapping Cooling-Off Periods**

* R5: **Device and Transaction Cooling Reconciliation:** To prevent catastrophic operational friction (e.g., a 52-hour total delay), the bank specifically addresses the interaction between new device enrollment and urgent wire transfers.  
* R6: **Concurrent Timers:** When a 48-hour new device cooling-off period overlaps with a 4-hour large remittance review window, the periods run concurrently, capping the total delay at 48 hours.  
* R7: **Emergency Override:** If a client must initiate an urgent wire from a newly enrolled device, the "In-Branch Compassion Override" allows a Branch Manager to verify the client's identity in person, immediately nullifying both the 48-hour device and 4-hour transaction cooling periods.

\*\*

**4\. Limits & Thresholds**

* Payroll/Gov Deposit Hold: 0 Days.  
* Standard Check Hold (\<$1500): 3 Business Days.  
* Large Check Hold (\>$1500): 5 Business Days.  
* Immediate Access Amount: $200.00 CAD.

**5\. Compliance and Risk**

### **VB-COMP-001: FINTRAC Reporting, AML, and Business Continuity**

**1\. Purpose**

To comply with Canadian Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations and ensure business continuity without imposing undue friction on legitimate community transactions.

**2\. Scope**

Cash deposits, international remittances, suspicious activity, and emergency authorizations.

**3\. Policy Rules: FINTRAC and AML**

* R1: **LCTR Compliance:** Any cash deposit or series of cash deposits by the same individual totaling **$10,000.00 CAD** or more within a 24-hour period automatically generates a Large Cash Transaction Report to FINTRAC.  
* R2: **EFT Reporting:** Electronic Funds Transfers (international remittances) exceeding $10,000.00 CAD are automatically reported to FINTRAC.  
* R3: Given the cash-heavy nature of certain working-class industries (e.g., landscaping, domestic work, tips), frequent sub-$10,000 cash deposits are considered normal behavior and do not automatically trigger a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) unless accompanied by attempts to obscure the source.  
* R4: "Structuring" or "Smurfing" (breaking up deposits to avoid the $10k limit) is monitored by automated AML algorithms.

**4\. Policy Rules: Signatures and Business Continuity**

* R5: **Digital Authorization in Emergencies:** Venus Bank recognizes that requiring physical "wet-ink" signatures from Managing Partners for major exceptions creates critical bottlenecks during emergencies. Under the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), Venus Bank utilizes legally enforceable Digital Multi-Factor Signatures (eSignatures) for all high-level exceptions.  
* R6: This entirely supersedes any "Cantonal Override" or wet-ink requirement, ensuring business continuity and shielding the bank from liability caused by delays if executives are physically unavailable during natural disasters or pandemic lockdowns.

### **VB-COMP-002: Prohibited Use, Assets, and Cryptography**

**1\. Policy Rules: Prohibited Use**

* R1: Venus Bank accounts may not be used to operate unlicensed Money Services Businesses (MSBs).

**2\. Policy Rules: Prohibited Assets and Cryptography**

* R2: **Prohibition of Cryptocurrency Collateral:** Venus Bank strictly adheres to warnings from federal regulators regarding crypto-asset risk management. Use of accounts for purchasing Cryptocurrency is strictly prohibited to protect vulnerable customers from crypto-investment scams and high volatility. Transactions to known crypto exchanges (e.g., Binance, Coinbase) are automatically blocked. We do not accept cryptocurrency as collateral at any LTV. By completely rejecting crypto collateral (e.g., refusing to offer 30% LTVs on Bitcoin), we avoid the need for high-frequency valuation methodologies, daily margin recalculations, and the requirement to hold additional Tier 1 capital against the extreme volatility risk of these assets.  
* R3: **Multi-Party Computation (MPC) and Collusion Prevention:** For internal banking cryptographic operations (securing customer data), we utilize MPC and FIPS 140-2 Level 3 HSMs. To ensure that geographically distributed participants are not subject to internal coercion or coordinated attacks, our ceremony participants are legally mandated to be from at least two independent organizations (e.g., Venus Bank executives and authorized representatives from an independent partner Credit Union). This true decentralization prevents internal collusion and mitigates the risk of single-jurisdiction legal compulsion.

**6\. Products and Credit Building**

### **VB-PROD-001: Credit-Builder Loans, Secured Cards, and Liquidations**

**1\. Purpose**

To help newcomers and unbanked individuals establish a Canadian credit score (Equifax/TransUnion) from scratch, without risking debt spirals or unfair operational timing constraints.

**2\. Scope**

"Stepping Stone" Secured Cards, "Credit-Builder" Micro-Loans, margin calls, and asset classifications.

**3\. Policy Rules: Credit-Building Products**

* R1: **Credit-Builder Loan:** The bank issues a small loan (e.g., $1,000 CAD). The funds are immediately locked in a CDIC-insured Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC). The customer makes monthly payments (e.g., $85/month for 12 months). Payments are reported to credit bureaus. Once paid off, the GIC unlocks, and the customer receives the $1,000 plus interest.  
* R2: LTV on Credit-Builder Loans is effectively 100% (fully cash-secured in trust).  
* R3: **Stepping Stone Secured Card:** Customers can place a minimum deposit of $200.00 CAD to receive a Visa credit card with a matching limit. No credit check is required.  
* R4: After 12 months of on-time payments, the Secured Card automatically upgrades to an Unsecured Card, and the initial deposit is refunded to the checking account.

**4\. Policy Rules: Margin Calls and Settlement Timelines**

* R5: **Operational Timing Mismatches:** A core tenet of Venus Bank is preventing procedural unfairness. A standard 4-hour margin call cure period can be operationally impossible if it overlaps with daily wire transfer cut-off times.  
* R6: **The 24-Hour Grace Alignment:** If a margin call occurs on a secured product (like a Credit-Builder loan) after 7:00 AM, or at a time when same-day wires cannot be executed to meet an 11:00 AM wire cut-off, the cure window is automatically extended to 24 hours (or the next business day's 11:00 AM clearing cut-off). This ensures that clients who must wire funds from external institutions are not prematurely and unfairly liquidated due to systemic timing mismatches.

**5\. Policy Rules: Asset Classification and Emergency Liquidity**

* R7: **Asset Classification (GICs vs. Allocated Gold):** Unlike institutions that utilize opaque "Allocated Gold" bailments in offshore vaults—raising questions about commodity price risk and creditor claims during bank insolvency—Venus Bank uses fully transparent CAD instruments. All collateral held for Credit-Builder Loans is placed in Guaranteed Investment Certificates (GICs). These are strictly classified as "On-Balance Sheet Custody Deposits" under OSFI capital adequacy rules. They are 100% CDIC-insured, ensuring clients possess direct ownership rights and absolute protection in insolvency scenarios.

\*\*

* R8: **Emergency Liquidity & Early Withdrawals:** We recognize that working families face acute financial crises. If a client faces a severe emergency, they are not strictly held to the "Hold-to-Maturity" requirement of their Credit-Builder GIC. Under the "Compassion Override", a Branch Manager can authorize the early, penalty-free withdrawal of the locked GIC funds to provide emergency liquidity. The client does not need to provide obscure "Force Majeure" documentation, nor are they charged disruption fees.

**6\. Limits & Thresholds**

* Credit-Builder Loan Range: $500.00 \- $2,500.00 CAD.  
* Secured Card Minimum Deposit: $200.00 CAD.  
* Interest Rate (Credit Builder): 4.5% APR.  
* Automatic Unsecure Upgrade: 12 Months of perfect payment history.

### **VB-PROD-002: "Roots" Non-SIN Mortgage Program**

**1\. Purpose**

To provide pathways to homeownership for Permanent Residents, work-permit holders, or ITIN holders (in cross-border scenarios) who lack deep Canadian credit history.

**2\. Policy Rules**

* R1: Down payment requirements are higher than traditional insured mortgages: A minimum of **20% down payment** is required.  
* R2: In lieu of a Canadian credit score, Venus Bank accepts **Alternative Credit Data**. This includes 12 months of consistent rent payments, utility bills, and international remittance histories.  
* R3: Maximum Gross Debt Service (GDS) ratio is 39%, and Total Debt Service (TDS) ratio is 44%.  
* R4: Multi-generational co-signing is permitted; up to 4 family members residing in the same household may combine incomes to qualify.

**3\. Limits & Thresholds**

* Min Down Payment: 20%.  
* Max LTV: 80%.  
* Max Co-Signers: 4\.

**8\. Exceptions and Governance**

### **VB-EXCP-001: The Compassion Override**

**1\. Purpose**

To empower local branch staff to make human-centric decisions when rigid policies fail a customer in crisis.

**2\. Scope**

Fee reversals, temporary hold lifts, emergency cash access, and emergency liquidity releases.

**3\. Policy Rules**

* R1: Community Branch Managers have the unilateral authority to waive any replacement card fee, wire fee, or lift a check hold up to $1,000.00 CAD if a customer presents a compelling hardship (e.g., sudden illness, job loss, domestic abuse situation).  
* R2: All Compassion Overrides are logged in the customer file but do not require corporate or "Head Office" wet-ink approval, allowing for immediate resolution of community crises.  
* R3: Overrides are capped at a maximum financial risk of $1,000.00 CAD per customer per year. Requests exceeding this require the approval of the Regional Community Director.  
* R4: Financial limits for Compassion Overrides are tracked per branch to ensure equitable distribution of financial relief across operating regions.

**4\. Limits & Thresholds**

* Branch Manager Override Limit: $1,000.00 CAD.  
* Fee Reversal Limit: Unlimited (at Manager's discretion).
